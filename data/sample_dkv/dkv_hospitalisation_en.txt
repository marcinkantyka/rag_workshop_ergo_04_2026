DKV BELGIUM — GENERAL TERMS AND CONDITIONS: HOSPITALISATION INSURANCE
Product: DKV Plus Hospitalisation | Version: 2024-01 | Language: EN

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ARTICLE 1 — PURPOSE OF THE CONTRACT

This insurance policy covers medical and hospitalisation expenses incurred by the insured as a result of illness, accident, or maternity, in accordance with the conditions defined in this document.

DKV undertakes to reimburse actual expenses up to the maximum amounts set out in the schedule of benefits annexed to this contract.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ARTICLE 2 — SCOPE OF COVERAGE

2.1 Territorial scope
Coverage applies to care received in Belgium and abroad, subject to the special provisions regarding care abroad (see Article 7).

2.2 Insured persons
The following persons are covered as expressly designated in the special conditions: the policyholder, their legal or cohabiting partner, and dependent children up to age 25.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ARTICLE 3 — COVERED HOSPITALISATION EXPENSES

3.1 Accommodation costs
DKV reimburses single-room accommodation costs up to EUR 150 per day. For a double room, reimbursement is capped at EUR 100 per day. Shared ward accommodation is reimbursed in full.

3.2 Medical fees
Fees charged by physicians, surgeons, anaesthetists, and other recognised practitioners are reimbursed based on the NIHDI (INAMI/RIZIV) rate, plus an honorary supplement capped at 200% of the agreed rate.

3.3 Medication and medical equipment
Medications prescribed during hospitalisation and necessary medical equipment are reimbursed at 100% of actual costs, with no excess.

3.4 Diagnostic examinations
Laboratory, radiological, and medical imaging examinations performed in a hospital setting are reimbursed in full.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ARTICLE 4 — EXCESS AND PERSONAL CONTRIBUTION

4.1 Annual excess
An annual excess of EUR 250 per insured person applies to hospitalisation costs. This excess rises to EUR 500 for hospitalisations related to elective pregnancy or non-reconstructive cosmetic surgery.

4.2 No excess for children
The excess does not apply to insured children under 16 years of age.

4.3 Personal contribution for single room
When hospitalised in a single room, the insured bears 15% of honorary supplements exceeding EUR 300 per stay.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ARTICLE 5 — STAY IN A NURSING HOME

5.1 Conditions for coverage
DKV covers nursing home (RVT/MRS) accommodation costs provided that:
- The preceding hospitalisation lasted at least 5 consecutive days
- Transfer to the nursing home occurs within 30 days of hospital discharge
- A medical certificate confirms the necessity of the placement

5.2 Maximum duration
Nursing home coverage is limited to 90 days per insurance year.

5.3 Reimbursed amount
DKV reimburses up to EUR 75 per day in a nursing home, after deduction of the compulsory health insurance contribution.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ARTICLE 6 — MATERNITY

6.1 Childbirth
Hospital childbirth costs are covered without excess, provided the insured has been enrolled in the contract for at least 10 months.

6.2 Pregnancy expenses
Antenatal consultations, ultrasound examinations (maximum 3 per pregnancy), and pregnancy-related blood tests are reimbursed at 80%.

6.3 Extended maternity stay
If a newborn requires extended neonatal care, DKV covers the mother's additional hospitalisation costs if she remains hospitalised for medical reasons.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ARTICLE 7 — CARE ABROAD

7.1 Emergency care
In the event of a medical emergency abroad, DKV reimburses medical and hospitalisation costs without limit, provided DKV is notified within 48 hours of admission.

7.2 Planned care abroad
Planned care in another EU country is reimbursed according to Belgian reference rates. Any costs in excess remain at the insured's expense.

7.3 Repatriation
Medical repatriation costs are covered up to EUR 10,000 per event.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ARTICLE 8 — EXCLUSIONS

The following are not covered:
- Aesthetic treatments with no reconstructive purpose
- Detoxification treatments not medically prescribed
- Dental care, unless the direct result of a covered accident
- Experimental treatments not recognised by NIHDI
- Accidents resulting from professional or high-risk competitive sport
- Pre-existing conditions not declared at the time of enrolment

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ARTICLE 9 — REIMBURSEMENT PROCEDURE

9.1 Claims notification
Every hospitalisation must be reported to DKV within 30 days of discharge, via the MyDKV portal or by registered post.

9.2 Required documents
- Hospitalisation certificate signed by the facility
- Settlement statement from the compulsory health insurer (mutuality)
- Original itemised invoices
- Medical report if requested by DKV

9.3 Processing time
DKV undertakes to process the claim within 15 business days of receiving all documents.
